GLOSS Terms Defined
12 terms

unit_count
Total STR properties a company manages. Primary input for lead value scoring. Populated by the pyairbnb scraper.
icp_unit_count
Subset of units that qualify as ICP — properties sleeping 8+ guests. A company might have 100 units but only 40 are ICP-grade.
lead_value
Commercial tier: High = 100+ ICP units, Mid = 30–99, Low = under 30. Primary outbound prioritisation field.
portfolio_type
Property quality tier: Luxe, Mid-Market, Budget, or Mixed. Affects which chef tier yhangry offers and how to pitch.
lead_source
Where a lead came from — e.g. "PMS - Hostaway US", "Conference: VRMA", "Em's LinkedIn". Determines messaging context.
Decision-Maker (DM)
✅ Include: CEO, Chief Executive Officer, Director (any), Owner, Founder, Co-Founder, President, Managing Director, MD, Property Manager, General Manager, CMO, Marketing Head/Manager/Director, Partnerships Head/Manager, Guest Experience Head/Manager.
❌ Exclude: Finance, CFO, Accounting, Bookkeeper, Operations Manager, Ops team, Housekeeping, Cleaning, Maintenance, HR, IT, Admin.
pyairbnb
Open-source Python library querying Airbnb's internal API. Pulls host data — property count, guest capacity, listing quality. Free, requires Python 3.11 + US VPN.
PMS
Property Management System — software (Hostaway, Guesty, Streamline etc.) used by STR operators. A company using a PMS is a confirmed STR operator — strongest ICP signal.
STR
Short-Term Rental — properties on Airbnb, VRBO etc. yhangry's partners manage multiple STRs (property managers), not solo hosts with 1–3 personal listings.
ICP
Ideal Customer Profile — a company managing 30+ STR properties in the US, including Luxe or Mixed-quality units sleeping 8+ guests.
GMAPS dump
Google Maps export uploaded Jan 30 2026. Brought in ~6,634 records, ~92% irrelevant (banks, universities, law firms). No lead_source applied at import.
Zero engagement
Contact where both hs_last_sales_activity_timestamp AND notes_last_contacted are blank — never emailed, called, or noted. 94.3% of contacts in the DB.
